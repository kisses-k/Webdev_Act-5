What is JavaCon Asia?

The premier gathering for Java enthusiasts in Asia!

JavaCon Asia brings together Java developers, enthusiasts, and industry leaders from across Asia for an exceptional conference experience. Hosted annually in a participating country, JavaCon Asia serves as a key platform for fostering collaboration, learning, and innovation in the Java ecosystem.

Past host countries include Singapore, Japan, Taiwan, South Korea, Malaysia, the Philippines, Thailand, and Indonesia. This year, we are excited to host JavaCon Asia 2025 in the Philippines, following the success of JavaCon PH 2024.

Building on the foundation of JavaCon PH—the largest gathering of Java developers in the Philippines—JavaCon Asia 2025 expands our reach to an international audience. The event fosters deeper collaboration and knowledge sharing across diverse cultures and industries throughout the Asia-Pacific region.

Meet Our Speakers

Learn from the best in the industry and gain insights from top experts in technology.

Dr. Venkat Subramaniam

Founder of Agile Developer, Inc

Dr. Subramaniam is an award-winning author and a globally recognized speaker known for his engaging sessions on Java and agile practices. He has delivered keynotes and talks at numerous conferences worldwide.

Brian Goets

Java Language Architect at Oracle

Brian is the author of "Java Concurrency in Practice" and has been instrumental in the evolution of the Java language. He frequently presents at major conferences, sharing insights on Java's future directions.

Josh Long

Spring Developer Advocate at VMware

Josh is a prominent figure in the Spring community, known for his contributions to Spring Boot and his "Bootiful" series of talks and workshops. He regularly speaks at global Java conferences.

Chandra Guntur

Distinguished Engineer at BNY Mellon

Chandra has been a technology leader in the financial services industry, focusing on Java application development and performance optimization. He is an active speaker at Java conferences and contributes to the Java community through various initiatives.​
